PiCloud

Description

PRIAn/IMT and PiCloud Inc, have signed an agreement to provide computational resources to the IMT community. This pilot effort, which commenced in June 2013, provided to all IMT faculty and students access to the impressive computational infrastructure of the PiCloud platform at a significantly reduced cost. This pilot case study intended to showcase the power and flexibility of the PiCloud Academic Program in helping academic insitutions meet their computational needs without costly investment in infrastructure and maintenance. Recently, PiCloud was acquired by Dropbox, but the platform continues as Multyvac and we continue to be significantly involved.
